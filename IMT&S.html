Wilmink Theatre Project

Educational point-and-click game designed to teach children theatre etiquette through interactive gameplay.

Overview

This project focuses on designing an educational game for children aged 5–10, introducing them to theatre spaces and house rules. The goal is to improve behaviour during school visits through engaging gameplay.

My Role

I worked on research, UI/UX design, and gameplay systems. My responsibilities included questionnaire design, playtesting coordination, prototyping, and contributing to the Game Design Document.

Design Process

The project followed the Double Diamond framework, focusing on problem exploration, user research, and iterative prototyping to align gameplay with educational goals.

Research & Testing

I conducted questionnaires with primary school children and analysed over 80 responses to understand preferences, engagement patterns, and gameplay expectations.

Prototyping

I created low and high fidelity prototypes in Figma and Visual Studio Code, testing gameplay interactions and refining mechanics based on feedback.

UI/UX Design

I designed intuitive UI systems tailored for children, focusing on clarity, accessibility, and engagement.

Gameplay Systems

Designed interactive minigames combining learning and play, including stage lighting mechanics and character customisation systems.

Reflection

This project strengthened my skills in UI/UX design, research, and teamwork. I improved my ability to balance educational goals with engaging gameplay.
